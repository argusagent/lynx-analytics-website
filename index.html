THE JOURNEY

Every model starts somewhere. Lynx launched in March 2026 with a multi-factor thesis: injury-adjusted spreads, rest advantages, sharp money signals, and public fade opportunities are consistently mis-priced by the market.

Each day is a feedback loop. Wins validate model assumptions. Losses get dissected — what data was stale, which factor was overweighted, where the edge was real vs. noise. That constant iteration is the whole point.

The target is 60%+ ATS win rate sustained over 100+ picks, with consistently positive Closing Line Value (CLV). CLV measures whether we got better numbers than the market closed at — a truer signal of model quality than any single game result.

This is a long game. The edge compounds. Follow the process.

Lynx is a machine learning model that processes live sports data every day to find games where the market has mispriced the spread. It accounts for team strength, injuries, rest, travel, and line movement - then surfaces only the picks where multiple independent factors align.

The model doesn't guess. It measures. Every pick comes with an edge estimate - the difference between Lynx's projected spread and the market line. The larger and more consistent the edge, the higher the confidence rating.

No pick is posted unless at least three independent factors agree. That quality gate is what separates a genuine edge from noise.

KEY INPUTS
Team Efficiency
Offensive and defensive efficiency ratings, adjusted for strength of schedule and recent form.
Injury & Roster Data
Real-time injury reports processed before every game. Player impact weighted by role and usage.
Rest & Schedule
Back-to-back games, road trip length, and days of rest between games - all factored into projections.
Home / Away Splits
Historical home and road performance for every team, updated throughout the season.
Line Movement
Opening lines vs. current lines. Sharp money moves markets - we track that signal as a confidence indicator.
Situational Context
Depleted rosters, schedule spots, and other situational flags that consistently influence outcomes.
